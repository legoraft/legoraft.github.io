Moving to a 'simple' Linux distro

January 9, 2023

Recently, I’ve switched to Pop!_OS on my laptop. It was quite a change for me, as I’ve always used Arch based distros as my default drivers, so switching to an Ubuntu based distro was something new. Even though I’ve loved the customization on my Arch install, I was very happy to have installed a distro that simply worked.

Why Pop

So System76 recently announced their work on a new desktop environment called COSMIC, which I was very interested in as they have a lot of good plans. The planned updates made me very interested in Pop, and given that I was very annoyed with my Windows 10 install, which was eating over 80% of my RAM in the desktop, I decided to switch to Pop even though they hadn’t even published an alpha for their new desktop environment.

The desktop environment Pop uses now is Gnome, I’ve already used Gnome in virtual machines, and it is one of my favorites, even though the other desktop environment I’ve used long-time is xfce with Manjaro, Gnome is the one I feel the most at home in.

The installation of Pop was quite easy, as was the installation of the apps I mostly use, it was almost all available in the Pop!_Shop.

Time management

After I installed Pop, it was just like running a normal pc. The thing I enjoyed most of having such a ‘simple’ or ’easy’ distro was the simplicity. I didn’t have to install a load of apps just to get my sound or USB working (I still need to manually mount my USB sticks on Arch) and it all worked fine. I recently wrote a blog post on how Linux can eat up your time and it makes it difficult to finish any type of normal work, but Pop did really fix a lot of the hassle I experienced by using a distro that makes you do everything yourself and install apps to do basic tasks.

I’m still very hyped for the new COSMIC desktop that System76 is planning, as it looks really good from what has been shown in blog posts and on Mastodon, so I’m going to sit on my current Pop!_OS 22.04 install and I will wait on the new COSMIC Desktop.

Final words

As I’ve said earlier, it was quite difficult to manage my time on my Arch install, because every time I started my computer, I could start on my never ending project of making my awesome config perfect or do some work. With this pop install, it really helped to just be doing work instead of working on a huge project.

I’ve also seen more desktop environments overhauling their UX and UI design, like Cinnamon, Unity and of course, Pop!_OS. This really could be a great year for the UX of Linux desktop environments, so I’m stoked for 2023.
